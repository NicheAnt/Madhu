Solo




On my own, not alone.
There is a deep wisdom that comes over time, only from the silence of solitude.



It’s a tricky place to be - in one’s head. Without initiation of some kind of ancient knowledge it can be hard. For those who have absorbed trauma of any form, without healing it can be almost impossible.



In order to be functioning for the good of the people around me and the environment I live in - I require regular solitude. To reflect, process, dissolve what harms and absorb what I learn. To heal and strengthen. To molt and grow. To create.



This is not an unusual demand. I believe it is a requisite for anyone to be healthy, any community to be healthy.



A lot of harm expressed, internally and externally, comes from not having processed something that has occured to a person. Knowing how difficult it can be when one is weakened or hurt, I also know the support and strength specific people can give you; like totems beaconing you back home to a safe and good place. To know when you need one, to know when to be one. I am learning how to carry that force with me where ever I go.
