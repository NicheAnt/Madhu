Growing






Green thumbs and beating hearts.
Gratefulness, gratitude.




The events have been recounted several times of the years. My grandmother would look after me when I was very young. My father, away at work and my mother, a teacher and my sister a student gone for the day at school. I honestly have no recollection of what scheming it took, but apparently when my grandmother would go in for her bath, I would lock the door half way through and run away into the gardens so I could play on my own, unsupervised. She would have to shout out to the gardner or the maid to come unlock her. This went on for several months, my grandmother trying all sorts of ways to avoid the imprisonment. Till finally everyone became okay with me playing without an overseeing eye. My mother would still get calls in the evenings saying that her daughter was scrounging in their garden or backyard. You see, they were all just one big connected land separated by hedges that would have gaps under them. I remember that.





Both my grandmothers had green thumbs and healing hands.






Friends, most recently - The summer of 2017 was spent labouring, readying, planning, sowing and tending to a new beautiful garden in Marpha. As part of the foundation’s plan to include a growing space as an extention of their teaching-learning programs. It was the first time I had the experience of watching such a range of bountiful plants and herbs grow from seed to harvest. In most rewarding climate, mineral and nutrient rich water and soil.



The process began with research and planning of our garden work, making hugelkulture beds, a nursery for the seedlings, drawing up plans for each bed according to favourably companion planting. We also planned our garden work - sowing, tranplanting, heavy watering and enriching according to the lunar cycles. What a gift to witness the transformation from bare land to lush and abundant greenary.



I am grateful for working so closely with the two other incredible humans whose first garden it was as well. Nishant and Jessica at the Foundation.




Green thumbs, like beating hearts do not rest.



My next garden - a balcony, a strip of earth, pots or someone else’s piece of land - awaits. I have started to dream of leaves.




And it is beginning at GoodEarth in Kengeri with the community shared gardens that the incredibly thoughtful architects included in the landscaping and designing of the space.
