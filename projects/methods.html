Practicing Invitation as an approach




Some guiding principles




: outward optimism

- human potential is always there, waiting to be discovered and invited forth
- to be inviting in a disinviting space is to change the environment
- challenges, problems and seeming impossibilities may be invitations in disguise




: sharing respect

- the price of any invitation is for the other person’s right to say no




: carrying trust

- an invitation is an opportunity to build trust
- to be inviting, it is necessary to trust the process
- trust is based on a memory of invitation sent, received and acted upon
- as trust develops, so does the nature of invitations




: intentionality

- an invitation is never an accident, it is a choice made, a choice someone took
- good intentions are not always good invitations
- when the opportunity is ripe, invite. there may not be another chance to wait for




* based on Laurie Frank’s Guiding Principles to Invitational Education
