Journey Woman



What are you?



A creative force of strength and care, connected to the natural cycles of the earth and moon, to the cycles of bith and death, or caring for and letting go, of incubating and forming. Of womb, of heart, of intuition and breath. Of hands, and feet, thighs and hips, of waists and breasts, of shoulders and neck, of hair.



Every soul, woman or man, must be allowed the freedom to explore their sense of gender-identity without being forced into categories that conform, or oppress.




There is a certain quality of a remarkable individual that transcends gender. One might express deep characteristics of feminity and masculinity in different fractions and forms, but it is a quality of grace to move fluidly between them. And this can allow to bloom a deep understanding of each other.




Many of us, however, are still reeling from years of deep hurt that runs several generations long of prepetrated wounds inflicted on the basis and bias of gender. Openly, there is the obvious truth that women are forced into innumerable and unsufferable situations of injustice.



Seemingly irrational violence always loops the thread of the question, 'Why?' right back to the history of the perpetrator.Whether the wounds are self-inflicted or externally initiated, the hurt is deep. So deep that the memory of the attacks are forgotten and only the vengence for repeating the hurt that is felt.




These are words of violence.







Words of Hope.



A piece of writing that has been guiding and revealing is the book The Women Who Run With The Wolves. The connections and stories and archetypes are rooted in culture much older than any written history.




It struck me how this knowledge is innate in most of us. It might be laying dormant, waiting for the right string of words, the melody of bones, the spirits of the forests - to stir it awake. The awakening preceeds an opening into a form of higher consciousness.




Energy work



I find this an integral part of my journey of discovery and understanding of people and phenomena. I hesitate to describe it in words as I am a novice, even if I intuitively feel I am deeply connected to healing practices.



I am still understanding the flow of energies; drawing and channeling strength. I am also aware of how much self-work it takes to become that vessel - to hold and allow things to flow, to not cling to singular ways of things that cause one to react, absorb and create knots in the body that block flow and eventually create pools of illness and dis-ease.




Knowing that is is wisdom that weaves through generations of practioners, I seek a mentor.
