It took me some years to understand and embrace that the work that truly excites and motivates me are community based collaborative work.
My sense of purpose in work comes from sharing a vision with individuals/group that involve an creative expression of ideology, imagination and compassion.


Collaborating with Tilo at Marudam school to experience the forest in new ways with a group of 6 students. Where we spent months using each sense to go out into the forest and then find ways of sharing what we experienced through descriptions, drawings, sculptures and collections.


Working with Jessica, the director of Marpha Foundation, has been an incredible relationship of mentor and collaborator that I am immeasurably grateful for.


Karishma, Malashree, Jolien, Maithreyi, Jazz, Nishant, Nikita, Aman - some of the many incredible educators who I have had the chance to work with over the years.


Planning, sowing and caring for a beautiful garden with Nishant. Teaching, working, making decisions of all sizes and sharing life journeys together.



And all the incredible women and artists I find myself journeying with since our time together at college - Ishita, Tanvee, Sindhu, Rajasee, Rucha, Avani, Ragini, Aman, Mahafrin, Alagu, Pooja, Varshita, Gauri, Natasha and Vasundhara - creators and practictioners of such interdisciplinary fields.
I am grateful for each strong individual that makes this tribe, this community that truly seems to rejuvinate all senses when we gather.
