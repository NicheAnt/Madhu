Forests





There is a war raging.
It feels like a non-dual phenomena - what we are subjecting natural and ecological systems of the earth to and the nature of illness and dis-ease in human bodies.



Looking inward to my own body’s response to illness, there is a consciousness that affects overall welbeing and the nature of safeguarding, protecting and generating the will to thrive, just as there is a consciousness that directs the body to get better in times of dis-ease. It is the nature, the innate, internal nature of certain cells to protect the body. An awareness of the larger systems that require a balance of that microscopic life in order to function, whose functioning in turn creates the environment for that microscopic life to exist. An integrated knowledge by virtue of existance, an awareness of interpependence of existence.



We need forests, living beings, natural water cycles, the deep stored pools of aquifers and oil to exist. These forms of life and form are so deeply intricate and interlinked. Yet, we are dismanteling billion year old systems at a pace of complete denial and no understanding.






I have memories from when I was a little younger than three. When we used to stay in a housing colony at the edge of the forest in a town called Sunabeda in Orissa. This was 1992-1993. I loved running below the hedges into everyone’s gardens, skirt the edge of the tree line on the other side of the road, wander just a little into the jungle.



My mother, as any sensible adult, tried to warn me of running off on my own. She would try and scare me saying that a bear would mistake me for one of its babies because of my big mop of black curls. I loved the forest as a wild baby would.




We did not stay there long, shifting away to Bangalore in 1994. Back when it was a small town, populated by more old trees than cars or people put together. Ah, those were the good days. A trail of memories from the wild lingeres as I would climb trees, rescue puppies and kittens in the monsoon, look out for nests in the neighbourhood trees, watch insects of all shapes and sizes crawl and fly, at their various stages of life.





Then the city swallowed it all.



It was not until I lived and worked with some truly remarkable individuals who are involved with reforestation that something so old, so true in me found life again. The beauty in all the complexity and balance, whose observation and attempt at understandin or absorbing will in itself reveal so much truth into the nature of the universe and ourself.





I am married.



To the idea, attempt, will and commitment of what it means to believe in all the connected forms of life in the wild, in forests and grasslands, deserts and oceans, frozen landscapes and freshwater.



Not viewing them as species to be protected or saved, isolated from the consequences of actions we commit. But as a part of a deep belief, an ideology of an experiential truth.
