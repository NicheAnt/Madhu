Love and Companions



‘There are places I remember All my life though some have changed Some forever not for better Some have gone and some remain All these places have their moments With lovers and friends I still can recall Some are dead and some are living In my life I've loved them all’ - In My Life, The Beatles




This is a love letter, a letter for love.




With each person I have been in a relationship with, I have known instantly that this human in front of me is going to be somehow significant in my life.



Intially platonic, something deep and intuitive. Fallen into that moment from different lifetimes. I feel my being pulled towards all of them. And at first, I feel as bold as love. Then, the hard work.




A relationship, to me, seems a reflection of oneself. The closer one is to another person, the more innards of tangled sinew and locked bones emerge. The gory bits of another, but more so - the gory bits of oneself. Blood on the hands.




With the beauty, blood. I know my hearts sings with the moments that I can see the delicate, strong, rooted and growing person that I am with. Humming with the lightness of laughter shared and sillyness witnessed. Skips, jumps and runs for the giddyness of love felt, love shown. Heart beat, hearts beat. But it can also carry the weight of torn words and insensitive gestures, ours both. Heavy with the the truth that there are parts of us that chafe at each other and we still have the stubborness of young hearts; two dogs at a bone not willing to give up their bite. I know I still have so many aspects of myself to understand and undo. So many parts that need healing from hurt - old, old wounds whose origins I may not even know. Most of the time, these wounds need a wolf’s nature to heal. Alone, on one’s own. In connection with the hum of the larger universe.



What I want to learn is the strength to walk with grace. To understand the timing of love better and be at peace with it. To be at ease with absence and the closeness of continuous presence. Feel the lifetime of a journey with a partner and recognise its death. And to also know that death renews another life. I perceive it as a dual nature now. One or the other, together or apart. With someone or alone. On my own, I am somehow able to let in a multitude of other relationships. I feel myself more open, inviting serendipititous and genuine encounters. Journeying souls crossing paths.



I know I need solitude from time to time, for phases in my life that I can only do on my own. The question is still murky, stretching without form between my fingers, how to balance returning to that one person and the direction of the larger questions in my life that will lead me to the people who will continue to stay important in my life. Collisions of spirits and souls, stars in orbit.
