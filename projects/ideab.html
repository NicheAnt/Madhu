ADULT GROUPS

: Turtle Soup

One person in the group decides a story narrative. And they present only the ending to the group(this can be a dramatic telling). Everyone else then begins to ask questions to the storyteller, who can only answer with a Yes/No/Maybe. At a given point, the teller will know when the group has pieced together the entire story.

The process can seem so serendipititious and in the flow of a group deciding a collective reality together.



: Eye Shut Disco

Foot stools, cushions or mats - place a number suitable for how many people there are, keeping some folks as audience. With a good music background, everyone on the given platform dances and moves to the next with their eyes closed. Talking aloud, confirming if they are able to go all the way to moving with their eyes completely closed. Individuals can switch as they wish.



: Energy Ball

Pass it in what ever form you allow yourself to create in that moment with gestures and even sound; receive it with awareness. Take time to silently reflect what individuals offered and in what manner they took.



: Hello, Stranger

In two lines facing each other, walk to the center to exchange glances, words, conversations and emotions - as strangers first, then aquiantences, to casual friends, to close ones. [from a workshop facilitated by Laurie Frank]



: List-o-mania

This one can be nice on walks. Everyone takes turns giving a list title; common or strange for everyone else to add to.
Eg: Good boy names for girls, Most risky situations they have been in, Oldest Memory they have, Best combination of three fruits, etc.




DUETS

: Book-Shelf Interventions

Ask a question to the other person. They have to listen and absorb the question before picking a book from the shelf, still keeping the question in their mind they open to a page at random and read a paragraph or the whole page. Discuss possible interpretations of what is read.



: Improv-orchestra

Sing together! Impromptu and totally improvised. Introduce the fictitious name and history of the song before starting.



: Would you rather..?

This or that! Take turns asking questions that have strange alternatives. Would you rather be as rich as you can imagine and never have the ability to experience laughter or would you rather travel the world as much as you want but not stay anywhere longer than six months?



: They are coming from

A game in a public space. Where you decide to create a story for where a stranger is coming from - picking details of their outfits, mannerisms to validate the thought.




ON YOUR OWN

: That year, this time

Choose a time of a season or a month and then pick a year. Try to recall what you could have been doing and if you have any visual or vivid memory of it.



: Body-ody-dy-y

Silence and a slow down. Focus on one part of your body with your eyes closed. Give it attention and give it love. Spread out limb to limb from there for a few minutes.




CONCEPTS FOR CLASS

: Name, Place, Animal, Food

Either choose a letter of the alphabet and research multiple options for it or one option each for each letter A-Z. Keep rich research material handy to look up and look at.



: Letter to a Country

Using the Name, Place, Animal, Food game as a base, everyone picks a country and writes a letter, song/poem/haiku/prose to someone from there. It can take the form of a video, a diorama, a shadow cut-out.



: Treasure Hunt

Clues and maps, questions and paths, people and mysteries. Generate the content prior to this activity/project from the group.



: Puppets

THEATRE

: If it was me

Create a list of situations that are tricky, naming the character in question first and the circumstances they are caught in.
Eg: Character - Neighbour
Situation - Getting caught picking up fallen
apples from your neighbour's garden!
Allow students to come up with situations for each other. You can give a list of characters to start with. Keep a time cap for the role play.
Use Freeze and Switch for the group to also see how different individuals respond to the same situation.



: Tracking/Stalking

Watch videos of the movement of certains animals (predator and prey) to observe in detail how limbs and muscle move, how the animal respond to movement, sound and light in their environment.
Split the group into two and assign them an animal for the exercise to follow. If you can choose a music piece to go with three different levels of movement - slow stalking, a quickened and alert pace and a fast chase.
Go through these in different orders, giving the students time to feel out how they want to express themselves. Catch a good ending where the energy is high but not exhausted.




NATURE BASED EXPERIENCES

: Sound Map

Divide a sheet of paper into 4 quadrents. Mark the center with a small circle. This represents the listener. Also mark North, South, East and West on the map accordingly (North being the line that moves up from the center).
With some research to where you are taking the group, prepare a few recordings to playback that are some of the sounds of the birds and animals in that region that they might here.
Do an exercise before they head out to sharpen their observation and prime their ability to diffrentiate sounds.
Once the walk commences, request silence. The exercise is to listen. Each child can choose a spot, within eye range of the others to sit and listen. Listen deeply to the sounds around them, closing their eyes at first.
Then slowly, they can start to map the sounds they hear. Representing on the map which direction, what intensity and maybe even drawing a wave that represents the pattern of the sound.

Once the exercise is over, each individual can share their map with the group and discuss any keen observations they had.
