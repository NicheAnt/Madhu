Tools and Methods in Practice -



: Outdoor and nature experiences

Opening, deepening connections with the natural world; creating exercises that deeply engage the senses through observation and forms of expressing the experience.
Designing curriculum that involves engaging with curiosity, investigating, experimenting, exercising creative thinking and assuming responsibility.



: Project based learning

An approach that starts at sources of strengths and stating a collective vision for the future, versus a problem-solving approach that begins with stating issues of concern.
Themes for appreciative-inquiry based curriculum can include not just science and environmental themes, but also as wide as gender, group dynamics, forms of expression, learning styles, decision making and execution among a few.



: Appreciative-Inquiry based curriculum

Stories are one of the oldest forms of communicating experience/learning. A living language that has many ancient roots and takes life in many forms. The art and craft of storytelling can be extremely powerful – engaging memory, senses, imagination and empathy.



: Storytelling, stories, puppets

Script characters stage props

All beginning with intentionality.
Theatre is one of the most powerful tools to use as a transformative process. It requires a guide to be entirely present, draw challenges while maintaining a safe space for a group to express vulnerebility and boldness.



: Theatre, LARP (Live Action Role Play)

Play opens, play unlocks.
It is said that play is one of the best ways to explore and discover the unknown; for the brain to form new neural pathways; to put in practice what one is learning; to observe and learn about another human or creature; to express some of the oldest ways of interacting as a group.



: Word games, physical games, activities, play

Observe, imagine, interpret, express, critique, absorb, transform. Transform perspective, transform opinion, transform expression.
Exercises in developing visual language and skills to choose and use one’s tools.



: Mindmaps, maps, comics

Mapping as a learning tool provides fantastic insight to subjects, the dynamics between connected topics and phenomena, the relationship of the learner to the subject, collective awareness of a group and so many more layers based on how the question is presented.
Comics often allow children to project their imagination, reality blended with fantasy. It is a great tool to introduce conceptual and abstract themes, allowing for a group to express individual perspectives.



: Exercises for creative visual-expression

Critical thinking is one of the most effective tools that a person can use to respond to things around them. Observation, examination/analysis and evaluation based on universal intellectual values. A crucial aspect is holding multiple perspectives, to form an opinion that transends divisions. This involves facilitating discussions and creating a space that holds respect for listening.



: Discussions, Interventions

Added to these is a sense of choreography that connects and opens channels – allowing for something authentic to emerge from the group.
