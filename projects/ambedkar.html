A personal photobook exploring constructed narrative through non-linear storytelling.



1984
My parents moved to Bangalore soon after their marriage, in 1984. Born and brought up here, in Bangalore, I have never felt fully connected to the politics of my roots in Tamil Nadu and the city in which my parents spent their youth - Chennai.



When I asked them about the dynamics of youth and politics in their time, they spoke about this sense of pride and nationalism, of a bigger feeling of optimism for change and a more confident faith in the system; when the country was new and forming and when the role of the people was not expendable.
Between my mother, the youngest of 6 siblings and my father, the eldest of 4, I have heard innumerable stories during their college years - of marches and strikes, petitions and walks, or debates and challenges, dangers and adventures.



Listening to those decades of movement and involvement, of generations that had not yet been disillusioned by cynicism or complete distrust in the system, I wonder where all that ideology and faith got misplaced.
And I realized that I am yet to make that journey to discover my own.
